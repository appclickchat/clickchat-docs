Child Safety Standards

Last updated: April 25, 2026

Zero Tolerance Policy: ClickChat (קליקצ'אט) has zero tolerance for Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) and Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). Any violation results in immediate account termination and reporting to the appropriate authorities.

1. Our Commitment

ClickChat is committed to providing a safe environment for all users, including teenagers, and to preventing the exploitation of minors. We comply with all applicable child safety laws, including the United States Code 18 U.S.C. § 2258A, COPPA, and GDPR-K, and we cooperate with global law enforcement efforts to combat CSAE and CSAM.

2. Age Requirement

ClickChat is intended for users aged 13 and older. Users must confirm they are at least 13 years old to register and use the service. Where local law requires a higher minimum age (for example, 16 in parts of the European Union), users must meet that higher threshold or obtain verified parental consent.

Accounts identified as belonging to users under the minimum age are immediately suspended and removed from the platform.

Parental Guidance

We strongly encourage parents and guardians of users aged 13–17 to:

  • Discuss safe online communication with their children
  • Review the app's privacy and safety features together
  • Report any concerning behavior immediately to our safety team

3. Prohibited Content and Behavior

The following are strictly prohibited on ClickChat:

  • Any sexual content involving or depicting minors (CSAM)
  • Grooming, solicitation, or sexual exploitation of minors
  • Sextortion or coercion targeting minors
  • Sharing, requesting, or producing CSAM in any form
  • Trafficking or facilitating the trafficking of minors
  • Adults seeking romantic or sexual relationships with minors
  • Sharing of personal contact information by or with minors
  • Any communication intended to harm, abuse, or exploit a child

4. Enhanced Protections for Minors (13–17)

Recognizing that teen users require additional safeguards, ClickChat implements the following protections for users aged 13–17:

  • Stricter content moderation: Lower thresholds for flagging and reviewing content involving accounts identified as belonging to minors.
  • Privacy by default: Personal information is hidden from other users by default for minor accounts.
  • Limited adult interactions: Where age signals are available, we restrict matching and messaging between adults and minors who are not known contacts.
  • Education prompts: In-app safety reminders about not sharing personal information, photos, or location with strangers.
  • Easy access to reporting and blocking tools in every conversation.

5. How We Prevent and Detect CSAE/CSAM

  • In-app reporting: Every user can report inappropriate behavior or content directly through the in-app report button.
  • Content moderation: We employ automated detection systems combined with human review to identify violations.
  • Account enforcement: Violating accounts are permanently banned, and the associated device/identifiers are blocked where technically possible.
  • Evidence preservation: When a violation is detected, relevant data is preserved for law enforcement.
  • Cooperation with authorities: We cooperate with NCMEC, INHOPE, and local law enforcement.

6. Reporting CSAE/CSAM

In-App Reporting

Users can report any chat or user violating our Child Safety Standards via the report button available in every conversation. Reports are reviewed promptly, typically within 24 hours.

External Reporting

To report CSAE/CSAM directly to ClickChat, contact us at the email below. To report directly to authorities:

  • NCMEC CyberTipline (US/Global): report.cybertip.org
  • INHOPE (international hotlines): inhope.org
  • Israel - 105 Hotline: National Online Protection Center

7. Legal Compliance

ClickChat complies with all applicable child safety laws in the jurisdictions in which we operate, including COPPA (US), GDPR-K (EU), and equivalent regulations. Suspected CSAM is reported to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and to regional and national authorities as required by law.
